## Personal Bio

Linhao Bai is a Georgia Tech MSCS student and STAR Lab research assistant working at the intersection of robot learning, dexterous manipulation, dynamics modeling, and real-world robotic systems. His recent work focuses on low-latency multi-modal inference, structured experiment logging, sim-to-real robustness, and long-horizon behavior modeling for manipulation.

Before Georgia Tech, he worked at SEER Robotics as a project manager and software engineer, delivering AMR software systems to international customers across North America, Europe, and Asia. That experience built a strong operating layer in deployment, reliability, telemetry, and customer-facing technical execution.

His broader technical profile combines research taste with systems pragmatism: robot learning, simulation, controls-adjacent engineering, and infrastructure that survives real deployment conditions.
